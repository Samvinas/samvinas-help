# PPCO

You'll see idea cards — ideas your group has chosen to make stronger. PPCO is
a way of developing an idea rather than debating it: first what's good, then
what it could become, then what worries you, and finally how to answer those
worries.

## How to take part

- Open an idea card and add your thoughts to its sections:
  - **Pluses** — what's genuinely good about this idea?
  - **Potentials** — what might it lead to if it worked?
  - **Concerns** — what worries you? Phrase it as a question:
    *"How might we…?"* A question invites an answer; a verdict doesn't.
- When you see a concern you can answer, add an **Overcome** under it — a
  concrete way of dealing with that specific worry.

## Why this order

Naming the pluses and potentials first isn't politeness — it earns the right
to be critical. By the time concerns arrive, the group is trying to *protect*
the idea's strengths, so criticism turns into problem-solving. Concerns
without overcomes are just complaints; this tool asks you for both.

> Your event may use different section names — the flow is the same:
> appreciate, imagine, question, answer.
