# PPCO — for facilitators

PPCO (Pluses, Potentials, Concerns, Overcomes) is the development-phase
workhorse of creative problem solving: a structured, *affirmative* way to
strengthen a promising idea instead of debating it. The group names what's
good, what it could lead to, what worries them — and then answers the worries.

It's the base of a family: **ALoU**, **LCO** and **PMI** run on the same
engine with different lenses. Reach for plain PPCO when you want the full
develop-and-overcome cycle.

## How to run it

- Pipe in the ideas you want to develop (for example, the top ideas from a
  vote). Each idea becomes a card.
- Participants open a card and add entries to three sections:
  - **Pluses** — what is genuinely good about the idea, right now.
  - **Potentials** — what it might lead to; futures it opens up.
  - **Concerns** — worries, phrased as questions ("How might we…?") so they
    invite answers rather than verdicts.
- Under each concern, the group adds **Overcomes** — concrete ways of
  answering *that specific concern*. Overcomes attach to the concern they
  answer, so the card keeps an honest map of problem → response.
- Developed ideas flow downstream: pipe PPCO into Voting or Ranking to choose
  again with clearer eyes.

## Configuring it

- The four section labels can be renamed per event in the tool's settings —
  useful for translation or house terminology. The order (affirm → imagine →
  question → answer) is the method; keep it.
- One PPCO instance handles many idea cards; you don't need a copy per idea.

## Facilitation tips

- Open with the **solution story**: before the sections fill up, have the
  idea's champion describe it in the present tense, as if it's already
  running — *"What I see us doing is…"*. The detail attracts genuine pluses
  and flushes out the real concerns.
- Enforce the order. The whole point is that concerns come *after* the group
  has invested in the idea's strengths — judgement becomes constructive
  instead of fatal.
- Insist concerns are questions. "It's too expensive" closes the topic;
  "How might we bring the cost down?" opens it.
- Not every concern needs an overcome — ask the group which concerns are
  load-bearing and spend the time there. Then take those **one at a time**: a
  card is done when every load-bearing concern has an overcome, or the group
  has consciously decided it can live with it.
