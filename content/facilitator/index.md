### What does it mean to be a facilitator?

You don't need a title or a certificate. A facilitator is the person who
looks after **how** the group works, so everyone else can pour themselves
into **what** it's working on. A few habits carry most of the weight — in a
Samvinas event or any meeting at all:

- **Guard the process; stay out of the content.** The problem belongs to the
  group; the way of working belongs to you. The moment you champion an idea —
  or even reword one — you've quietly become a judge, and deferred judgement
  stops being credible. If an idea of yours won't let go, say out loud that
  you're stepping out of the facilitator role for a moment, offer it once
  without selling it, and step back in.
- **Be the guide by the side, not the sage on the stage.** (A rhyme owed to
  educator Alison King.) The group doesn't need your answers; it needs your
  structure. Ask the question, set up the activity, and let the room do the
  thinking — conclusions a group reaches itself are the only ones it keeps.
- **Neutral doesn't mean passive.** Staying out of the content doesn't mean
  fading into the wallpaper. Run the room actively on the group's behalf:
  protect the quiet, connect the strangers, and check status at the door —
  in here, the intern's idea and the director's idea weigh the same. Clear
  rules, said out loud, make people freer, not less.
- **Make participation full, not merely open.** "Anyone can speak" quietly
  becomes "the confident speak." In open discussion only one person can talk
  at a time, and everyone else's thinking stalls while they wait —
  researchers call it *production blocking*, and it's why every Samvinas
  generating tool lets the whole room write at once. Start topics with
  silent writing, invite quieter people by name (with a free pass), and when
  one view is rolling, ask *"who sees it differently?"*
- **Respect different ways of working.** Some people talk in order to think;
  others think before they'll talk. Some light up generating ideas; others
  come alive clarifying, or planning. Alternate solo, pair and whole-group
  moments, share the challenge ahead of the day so reflectors arrive ready,
  and hold a few seconds of quiet after a question — silence is thinking,
  not failure.
- **Keep the ground rules, and the group's words.** You are the keeper of
  [deferring judgement](/principles/deferring-judgement.html) while the
  field is open and of
  [affirmative-first choosing](/principles/convergence.html) when it
  narrows. And capture ideas in the contributor's own words — rewording is
  judgement in disguise. If an idea runs long, ask its owner for the
  headline.

These habits are the human half of the craft; the
[principles refresher](/principles/) and
[the process at a glance](/principles/process-at-a-glance.html) cover the
process half.

## The toolbox, by what each tool does

Samvinas groups its tools the same way the app's tool picker does — by the
kind of move they make in a creative process:

- **[Generating tools](/facilitator/tools-generating.html)** — widen the
  field: produce many ideas and options before any judging starts.
- **[Focusing tools](/facilitator/tools-focusing.html)** — narrow down:
  compare, weigh and choose among the options you have.
- **[Organising tools](/facilitator/tools-organising.html)** — make sense:
  group, map, sequence and develop what you have, without cutting anything.

A well-shaped session usually alternates: diverge with a generating tool,
then organise or converge with what came out — and repeat.

## Structure & support

Three tools shape the event itself rather than the ideas in it:

- **[Breakout Groups](/facilitator/breakout-groups.html)** — split into small
  groups, each with a shared workspace.
- **[Personal Workspace](/facilitator/workspace-launcher.html)** — give every
  participant their own mini-flow.
- **[Instructions](/facilitator/instructions.html)** — a page of guidance or
  information for participants, right in the flow.

## Utilities

Utilities sit alongside the flow — enable them once and they're available
across every tool in the event:

- **[Timer](/facilitator/timer.html)** — a shared countdown in everyone's
  footer, with a fullscreen presenter mode on any device.
- **[Raise Hand](/facilitator/raise-hand.html)** — a fair, visible queue for
  speaking up.
- **[Event Info](/facilitator/event-info.html)** — a standing page for WiFi,
  schedule and venue details.

## Getting your data out

- **[The event report](/facilitator/event-report.html)** — the whole event as
  a single shareable HTML file, formatted for reading.
- **[CSV export](/facilitator/csv-export.html)** — every tool's results as a
  spreadsheet-ready file: the format, the columns per tool, and how to open
  it in Sheets, Excel or anything else.
