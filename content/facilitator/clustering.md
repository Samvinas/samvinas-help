# Clustering — for facilitators

Clustering makes sense of a big idea list by grouping related items into named
themes. It's a natural bridge from divergence to convergence.

## How to run it

- Pipe in the ideas (e.g. from Brainstorm).
- Let the group select and group items; encourage clear names.
- Groups collapse by default — expand to review them.

## Facilitation tips

- Clustering is social, and a few people often lead. Invite quieter voices to
  propose groupings.
- Don't over-cluster — 4–7 themes is usually more useful than fifteen.
- **Restate each cluster before moving on.** A name that *does* something — a
  verb phrase ("pilot the scheme with two teams"), or a fresh "How might
  we…?" if you're clarifying — turns a pile into a proposal. One-word labels
  waste the work the group just did.
- The named groups can pipe onward, so you can vote on or score whole themes.

## Piping onward

Clusters are category items; grouped items keep their link back to the original
idea, so downstream tools can work with either the themes or the underlying
ideas.
