# PMI — for facilitators

PMI (Plus, Minus, Interesting) is Edward de Bono's quick, balanced scan of an
option. Instead of reacting for or against, the group deliberately looks in three
directions: what's good, what's poor, and what's simply **interesting** —
worth noting even though it's neither good nor bad.

The "Interesting" column is the whole trick: it parks judgement for a moment and
surfaces implications and questions people would otherwise miss.

## How to run it

- Pipe in the options to scan. Each becomes a card with three sections:
  - **Plus** — the good points.
  - **Minus** — the weak or problematic points.
  - **Interesting** — anything noteworthy, curious, or "what if…" about it.
- PMI is fast — use it to open up a considered discussion, not to score a winner.

## Facilitation tips

- Insist on the Interesting column; it's the part that changes minds.
- Keep it quick — a few minutes per option is enough.
- Follow PMI with a focusing tool (Voting, Evaluation Matrix) if you need a choice.
