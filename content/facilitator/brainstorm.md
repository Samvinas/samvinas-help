# Brainstorm — for facilitators

Brainstorm is your idea‑gathering tool: a shared, live list everyone contributes
to at once. It's usually the opening, **divergent** step of a flow.

## How to run it

- Set a clear, open **Prompt** — "How might we…?" questions work well.
- Keep it visible and unlocked while ideas flow. Encourage quantity out loud.
- Watch the list; if it stalls, offer a fresh angle or a provocation.
- When you're ready to converge, **Lock** it and pipe the ideas into the next
  tool (e.g. Voting, Clustering, or Value/Doability).

## Facilitation tips

- Remind the group of the ground rules — defer judgement, go for quantity,
  welcome wild ideas, build on others. (Participants see these on their own
  help page.)
- Silence is fine; give people time to think. A minute of quiet often produces
  the best ideas.
- If a few voices dominate, Brain Writing or Breakout Groups can spread
  participation.

## Piping onward

Brainstorm produces `idea` items. Any downstream tool can consume them — add the
next tool and connect its **Source** to this one, optionally filtering by votes
or sequence range.
