# ALoU — for facilitators

ALoU is a way to weigh up a promising option in a constructive, balanced way. For
each idea the group looks at its **Advantages**, its **Limitations**, and its
**Unique Features** — and then works to **overcome** the most important
limitations rather than letting them sink the idea.

It runs on the same engine as PPCO; the difference is the lens. ALoU is good when
you want people to appreciate what is distinctive about an option, not only its
pluses and problems.

## How to run it

- Pipe in the ideas you want to develop (for example, the winners of a vote).
- Each idea becomes a card. Participants open a card and add to three sections:
  - **Advantages** — what is genuinely strong about it.
  - **Limitations** — stated as questions ("How might we…?") so they can be
    answered.
  - **Unique Features** — what is original or distinctive here.
- Under each limitation, the group adds **Overcomes** — concrete ways to answer
  that specific limitation.
- Developed ideas flow on: pipe ALoU into Voting or Ranking to re-choose after
  the analysis.

## Facilitation tips

- Insist that limitations are phrased as questions. "It's too expensive" stops
  the conversation; "How might we bring the cost down?" invites an overcome.
- Don't skip Unique Features — it is the step people forget, and it often surfaces
  the real reason an idea is worth pursuing.
- You don't need to overcome every limitation — focus on the ones that matter.
