# Backcasting — for facilitators

Backcasting starts from a **desired future** and works backwards. Instead of
asking "what should we do next?", the group pictures success and asks "for that
to be true, what must have happened just before — and before that?" — laying the
steps onto a rough timeline from now to the goal.

This tool handles the sequencing step: the group places each step into one of
ten time blocks (0 = earliest, 9 = just before the future). It's a rough sense
of order, not a project plan.

## Set it up

1. First, help the group converge on the **desired future state**, then run a
   brainstorm — "In order to have achieved this, what must have happened?" — and
   optionally tidy the steps with Voting or Clustering.
2. Pipe those steps into Backcasting. In its settings, paste the **desired
   future state** (it shows as the anchor at the top) and set the number of time
   blocks if ten isn't right.

## Run it

Work through the steps with the whole group. A good prompt: "What are the
immediate antecedents of the future state?" — those go in block 9. Then work
back. Each step has a dropdown (0–9, or "–" to leave it unplaced); anyone can
set or change any step, and everyone sees the board update live. Unplaced steps
sit in a pool at the top.

## What you can do next

Because each step is tagged with its block, you can **pipe a single block
onward**: send all the "0" steps into a Sign Up tool to find owners, or the "5"
steps into Elaboration to flesh them out. Add the downstream tool, pipe from
Backcasting, and pick the block in the pipe's filter.

## Tips

- Keep it rough — you're after the shape of the sequence, not exact timing.
- Not every step has to be placed; the unplaced pool is fine to leave.
