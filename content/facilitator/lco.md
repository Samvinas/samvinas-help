# LCO — for facilitators

LCO is an affirmative way to react to an option: the group notes what they
**Like**, raises **Concerns** as questions, and captures the **Opportunities** the
option opens up. It starts positive on purpose — people engage more when their
first move is to build, not attack.

Unlike PPCO or ALoU, LCO doesn't answer its concerns in place. Instead the
**Opportunities** feed the next round of ideation: pipe LCO onward into a fresh
Brainstorm and let the group generate new alternatives from what they found.

## How to run it

- Pipe in the ideas you want to react to. Each becomes a card with three
  sections:
  - **Likes** — what appeals about it.
  - **Concerns** — worries, stated as questions ("How to…?").
  - **Opportunities** — new possibilities or directions it suggests.
- Then pipe LCO into a Brainstorm to turn the opportunities (and the questions)
  into new ideas.

## Facilitation tips

- Do the Likes first and genuinely — the affirmative start is the point.
- Phrase concerns as questions so they become springboards, not dead ends.
- Treat Opportunities as the real payoff: they are where the next ideas come from.
