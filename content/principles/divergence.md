# Divergence

Divergence is the widening move: generating many options — ideas, questions,
interpretations, possibilities — before evaluating any of them. It's the
half of creative work that most groups under-do, because everyday
conversation rewards being right, and divergence asks you to be *plentiful*
instead.

## The four ground rules

1. **Defer judgement.** Nothing is criticised while the field is opening —
   see [deferring judgement](/principles/deferring-judgement.html).
2. **Go for quantity.** The first ideas are the obvious ones; the interesting
   material usually arrives after the group thinks it's finished. A long list
   is the raw material good choices are made from.
3. **Welcome wild ideas.** It's easier to tame a wild idea than to invigorate
   a timid one. The impractical often carries the seed of the practical.
4. **Build on the ideas of others.** "Yes, and…" — treat every contribution
   as a springboard, not a rival.

## What it looks like in Samvinas

The **[generating tools](/facilitator/tools-generating.html)** — Brainstorm
and its relatives — are built for this move: everyone contributes at once,
everything is visible, nothing is scored yet.

Divergence only earns its keep if it's followed by its partner:
**[convergence](/principles/convergence.html)**.
