# Deferring judgement

The single most important habit in creative work: while options are being
generated, **evaluation waits**. Not forever — judgement gets its own turn,
with better material to work on — but *later*, and everyone knows it.

## Why it matters

- **Judgement is a tax on contribution.** If ideas are evaluated as they
  arrive, every person runs their next thought past an internal censor first.
  The censor is conservative; the group gets safe, similar ideas — or
  silence.
- **Early ideas are scaffolding.** Many good ideas are only reachable *via*
  a mediocre one someone floated without embarrassment. Kill the mediocre
  idea on arrival and the good one never gets built.
- **"That won't work" is usually premature.** At the generating stage nobody
  has tried to make it work yet — that's what the develop stage
  ([PPCO](/facilitator/ppco.html) and friends) is for.

## How to actually do it

- Separate the phases visibly: *now we generate, later we choose*. A shared
  [timer](/facilitator/timer.html) helps mark the boundary.
- Notice the polite forms of judgement — "interesting, but…", the raised
  eyebrow, instant feasibility questions. They count.
- When evaluation does arrive, make it affirmative:
  [convergence](/principles/convergence.html) starts from what's promising.

Deferring judgement is one half of a pair; the other is
[framing problems as questions](/principles/problems-as-questions.html) — the
habit that keeps judgement constructive when it *is* time to judge.
