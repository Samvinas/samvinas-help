# The process at a glance

Creative Problem Solving is usually drawn as
[four stages](/principles/four-stages.html) — Clarify, Ideate, Develop,
Implement — but in practice the work breaks into **six steps**: Clarify is big
enough to take three of them. This page lays all six side by side for one
specific job: **working out where to enter**.

Because you don't start at step one. You
[assess the situation](/principles/four-stages.html) — what does the group
already have? — and enter at the step whose starting condition matches. Read
down the *Start here when…* column until a row sounds like your situation.

| Step | Start here when… | Sounds like… | You leave with |
|------|------------------|--------------|----------------|
| **1 · Frame the goal** (Clarify) | you want to create, improve or invent something, but it isn't defined yet | *"I wish…"* · *"It would be great if…"* | a goal, wish or challenge the group owns |
| **2 · Gather data** (Clarify) | you have a goal but haven't explored the territory around it | *"What do we know?"* · *"What have we tried?"* | the key facts and feelings that shape the challenge |
| **3 · Find the problem** (Clarify) | you want to pinpoint the best problem to solve | *"How might we…?"* · *"In what ways might we…?"* | a well-defined question naming the problem worth solving |
| **4 · Generate ideas** (Ideate) | you have a clearly defined problem and need ideas for it | *"What might be all the ways to…?"* | a shortlist of ideas the group wants to pursue |
| **5 · Develop solutions** (Develop) | you have promising ideas that need to become workable | *"What I see us doing is…"* | a developed, detailed solution |
| **6 · Plan for action** (Implement) | you have a solution and need buy-in and a path | *"Who does what by when?"* | a concrete plan of action |

Inside every step, the
[diverge–converge heartbeat](/principles/divergence.html) runs at least once:
widen first, then choose. Here's what that looks like per step.

## 1 · Frame the goal

Diverge on **wishes**: ambitions, irritations, daydreams — *"I wish…"*,
*"It would be great if…"*. No solutions yet, just appetite. Then converge on
a challenge that passes three tests: the group **cares** about it, can
**act** on it, and it genuinely **needs new thinking** — if a known fix
exists, go do that instead.
*Samvinas tools:* a [Brainstorm](/facilitator/brainstorm.html) of wishes,
[Word Cloud](/facilitator/wordcloud.html).

## 2 · Gather data

Diverge on the territory: What's the story so far? What's already been
tried? Who's involved, and how do they feel about it? What would ideal look
like? Then converge on the handful of findings that **change how you see the
challenge** — those travel with you through the rest of the process.
*Samvinas tools:* [Brainstorm](/facilitator/brainstorm.html) as a data
harvest, [Five Whys](/facilitator/five-whys.html) to dig under the surface.

## 3 · Find the problem

Diverge on **question framings**: turn what you learned into many candidate
problems, phrased as invitations — *"How might we…?"*, *"In what ways might
we…?"*, *"What might be all the…?"*. Ask *"why?"* to go broader and *"what's
stopping us?"* to go narrower (see
[framing problems as questions](/principles/problems-as-questions.html)).
Then converge on the question that names the problem actually worth solving —
often not the one you walked in with.
*Samvinas tools:* [Five Whys](/facilitator/five-whys.html), a
[Brainstorm](/facilitator/brainstorm.html) of questions, then
[Voting](/facilitator/voting.html) to pick the frame.

## 4 · Generate ideas

Diverge against the clarified question, with the
[ground rules](/principles/divergence.html) in force — judgement deferred,
quantity first, wild welcomed, building encouraged. Push past the first
wave: the obvious ideas come early, the interesting ones after the group
thinks it's finished. Then converge by
[highlighting](/principles/convergence.html): mark what stands out, cluster
it, restate it.
*Samvinas tools:* the
[generating tools](/facilitator/tools-generating.html), then
[Voting](/facilitator/voting.html),
[Ranking](/facilitator/ranking.html) or
[Clustering](/facilitator/clustering.html).

## 5 · Develop solutions

Take the shortlist and make it strong. Start by telling the **solution
story**: describe the idea in the present tense, as if it's already running —
*"What I see us doing is…"*. Detail attracts strengths and flushes out real
concerns. Then work it with [PPCO](/facilitator/ppco.html): pluses,
potentials, concerns phrased as questions — and an overcome for each concern
that matters, taken one at a time.
*Samvinas tools:* [PPCO](/facilitator/ppco.html) and its
[ALoU](/facilitator/alou.html) / [LCO](/facilitator/lco.html) /
[PMI](/facilitator/pmi.html) relatives,
[Elaboration](/facilitator/elaboration.html),
[Evaluation Matrix](/facilitator/evaluation-matrix.html).

## 6 · Plan for action

Diverge on actions: everything that might move the solution forward — steps
to take, ways to make it easy to understand, ways to show its advantages,
people whose acceptance it needs. Then converge on the key actions and pin
them down: **who does what by when**.
*Samvinas tools:* [Backcasting](/facilitator/backcasting.html),
[Sign Up](/facilitator/signup.html),
[Stakeholder Map](/facilitator/stakeholder-map.html).
