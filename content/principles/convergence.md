# Convergence

Convergence is the narrowing move: taking the field of options a group has
generated and choosing what to carry forward. It is not the opposite of
creativity — it's the half that turns creativity into outcomes. Done badly,
it undoes the divergence that preceded it; done well, it leaves the group
confident in its choices *and* glad it generated wildly.

## Principles for converging well

1. **Be affirmative first.** Start from what's promising, not what's wrong.
   Screening for fatal flaws comes later; strengthening comes first (this is
   what [PPCO](/facilitator/ppco.html) is for).
2. **Be deliberate.** Use explicit criteria, or explicit preferences —
   voting, ranking, scoring — rather than whoever talks longest.
3. **Check the wild ones before discarding them.** Novelty is exactly what
   you diverged for; don't let "unusual" be read as "unworkable".
4. **Don't converge too early.** The commonest failure: the group grabs the
   first workable idea and stops. If the list still feels obvious, diverge
   again.

## What it looks like in Samvinas

The **[focusing tools](/facilitator/tools-focusing.html)** — Voting, Ranking,
Value/Doability, Evaluation Matrix — make converging visible and shared,
instead of something that happens in the loudest corner of the room.

Convergence works on what divergence provides:
**[divergence](/principles/divergence.html)** comes first.
